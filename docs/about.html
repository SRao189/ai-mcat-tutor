I’m a student interested in medicine, education, and the practical use of artificial intelligence. My work focuses on understanding how AI can become more than a chatbot, how it can act as a reliable learning partner that explains difficult concepts, adapts to students, and helps them reason through problems.

I’m currently developing an AI-powered MCAT learning platform that transforms source material into structured lessons, worked examples, practice questions, and interactive chapter previews. A major part of the project is trust: the system checks whether lesson files are current, whether citations point to real source material, and whether numerical claims, units, equations, and worked-example answers are actually supported.

The project combines education, software design, research, and multi-agent AI workflows. My long-term goal is to help build learning tools that make high-quality education more accessible, understandable, and accountable.

What I am building toward

  • AI systems that explain instead of merely answer
  • Learning experiences that adapt without hiding uncertainty
  • Tools that treat verification and accountability as product features
