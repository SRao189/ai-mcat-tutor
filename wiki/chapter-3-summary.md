# Chapter 3: Biochemistry Basics — Summary

## Overview
This chapter covers foundational biochemistry concepts tested on the MCAT: thermodynamics, kinetics, redox reactions, and acid/base chemistry.

---

## Major Topics

### 1. [[thermodynamics]]
- **Gibbs free energy:** ∆G = ∆H – T∆S
- **Spontaneity:** ∆G < 0 (exergonic) vs ∆G > 0 (endergonic)
- **Standard free energy:** ∆G°′ = –RT ln K'<sub>eq</sub> (pH 7, 1 M solutes)
- **Actual cellular ∆G:** ∆G = ∆G°′ + RT ln Q
- **Le Châtelier's principle:** Q vs K<sub>eq</sub> drives reaction direction
- **Key:** Two factors determine spontaneity in cells — intrinsic properties (K<sub>eq</sub>) + concentrations (RT ln Q)

### 2. [[kinetics]]
- **Thermodynamics** ≠ **Kinetics** (∆G says nothing about rate)
- **Activation energy (E<sub>a</sub>):** Barrier to reaching transition state [TS]‡
- **Catalysts/Enzymes:** Lower E<sub>a</sub> by stabilizing TS, **do not change ∆G or K<sub>eq</sub>**
- **Enzymes:** Biological catalysts, kinetic role only

### 3. [[oxidation-reduction]]
- **Oxidation** = loss of e⁻/H / gain of O
- **Reduction** = gain of e⁻/H / loss of O
- **Redox pairs:** One oxidized, one reduced (always coupled)
- **Metabolism:** Catabolism = oxidative (releases energy); Anabolism = reductive (requires ATP)
- **Glucose oxidation:** C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂ → 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O

### 4. [[acids-bases]]
- **Brønsted-Lowry:** Acid = H⁺ donor, Base = H⁺ acceptor
- **Lewis:** Acid = e⁻ pair acceptor, Base = e⁻ pair donor
- **Conjugate pairs:** Differ by one H⁺
- **Strength:** Lower pK<sub>a</sub> = stronger acid; Lower pK<sub>b</sub> = stronger base
- **Amphoteric:** Can act as acid or base (e.g., HCO₃⁻, amino acids)
- **pH = –log[H⁺]; pH + pOH = 14 (at 25°C)**
- **Buffers:** Weak acid + conjugate base resist pH change
- **Bicarbonate buffer:** CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻ (key in blood)

## Key Equations to Memorize

| Equation | Use |
|----------|-----|
| ∆G = ∆H – T∆S | Thermodynamic spontaneity |
| ∆G°′ = –RT ln K'<sub>eq</sub> | Standard free energy ↔ equilibrium |
| ∆G = ∆G°′ + RT ln Q | Actual cellular ∆G from concentrations |
| pH = –log[H⁺] | Acidity |
| pH + pOH = 14 | Acid-base relationship (25°C) |
| pK<sub>a</sub> = –log K<sub>a</sub> | Acid strength |
| pK<sub>b</sub> = –log K<sub>b</sub> | Base strength |

---

## High-Yield Facts for MCAT

1. **ATP** = primary energy currency (high-energy phosphate bonds)
2. **∆G < 0** = spontaneous; **∆G = 0** = equilibrium
3. **Enzymes** lower E<sub>a</sub>, **do not** change ∆G or K<sub>eq</sub>
4. **Catalysts** speed up both forward and reverse reactions equally
5. **Redox:** OIL RIG (Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain)
6. **pK<sub>a</sub>:** Lower = stronger acid; Higher = weaker acid/more basic
7. **Bicarbonate buffer** = main blood buffer; CO₂ accumulation → pH drops
8. **Amphoteric** substances (HCO₃⁻, amino acids) can donate or accept H⁺
