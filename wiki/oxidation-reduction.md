# Oxidation and Reduction (Redox)

## Core Concept

**Oxidation** = loss of electrons
**Reduction** = gain of electrons

**Mnemonic:** OIL RIG — Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain

When one atom is oxidized, another **must** be reduced → **redox pair**

## Three Ways to Identify Redox Reactions

| Oxidation (loss) | Reduction (gain) |
|------------------|------------------|
| Gain of O atoms | Loss of O atoms |
| Loss of H atoms | Gain of H atoms |
| Loss of electrons | Gain of electrons |

## Quick Examples

| Change | Classification | Reason |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| CH₃CH₃ → H₂C=CH₂ | Oxidation | Hydrogens removed |
| Fe³⁺ → Fe²⁺ | Reduction | Electron added |
| O₂ → H₂O | Reduction | Hydrogens added to O₂ |
| Disulfide bond formation | Oxidation | Hydrogens removed |

## Energy Metabolism Context

**Photosynthesis:** Plants (photoautotrophs) store solar energy in carbohydrates
**Cellular respiration:** Animals (chemoheterotrophs) oxidize reduced molecules (carbs, fats) → CO₂ + ATP

**Glucose oxidation:**
```
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂ → 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O
```
- Carbons in glucose → oxidized (to CO₂)
- Oxygen → reduced (to H₂O)
- Redox pair: C (oxidized) / O (reduced)

## Catabolism vs. Anabolism

| Process | Direction | Redox | Energy |
|---------|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Catabolism** | Breaking down | Oxidative | Releases energy |
| **Anabolism** | Building up | Reductive | Requires energy (ATP) |

- Fatty acid synthesis = successive reductions of carbon chain
- ATP stores energy from catabolism; drives anabolism
