# Thermodynamics

## Key Concepts

**Energy Forms:**
- **Kinetic energy** ‚Äî movement of molecules
- **Potential energy** ‚Äî energy stored in chemical bonds
  - Most important potential energy storage molecule: **ATP** (energy in ester bonds between phosphate groups)

## Laws of Thermodynamics

**First Law (Conservation of Energy):**
- Energy of the universe is constant
- When system energy decreases, surroundings energy increases, and vice versa

**Second Law (Entropy):**
- Disorder (entropy, S) of the universe tends to increase
- Spontaneous reactions increase universal disorder
- ‚àÜS = S<sub>final</sub> ‚Äì S<sub>initial</sub>
- If ‚àÜS is negative ‚Üí disorder decreased

## Gibbs Free Energy (‚àÜG)

**Equation:** ‚àÜG = ‚àÜH ‚Äì T‚àÜS

Where:
- ‚àÜH = enthalpy change (bond energy + P‚àÜV)
- T = temperature (Kelvin)
- ‚àÜS = entropy change

**In cells:** Since ‚àÜV ‚âà 0 (liquid phase), **‚àÜH ‚âà ‚àÜE** (bond energy)

**Spontaneity:**
- ‚àÜG < 0 ‚Üí spontaneous (exergonic, energy exits system)
- ‚àÜG > 0 ‚Üí nonspontaneous (endergonic, requires energy input)
- ‚àÜG = 0 ‚Üí equilibrium

**Enthalpy:**
- ‚àÜH < 0 ‚Üí exothermic (liberates heat)
- ‚àÜH > 0 ‚Üí endothermic (requires heat input)
- Most metabolic reactions are exothermic ‚Üí maintains body temperature

**Sign convention:** All from system's perspective (negative ‚àÜG = system goes to lower free energy)

## Reaction Quotient (Q) vs. Actual ‚àÜG

**Equation:** ‚àÜG = ‚àÜG¬∞‚Ä≤ + RT ln Q
